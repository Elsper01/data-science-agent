summary="This dataset provides a longitudinal analysis of tourism activity in the municipality of Rettenberg, Germany, spanning the years 2014 to 2024. It is derived from the 'Monthly Tourism Survey' and focuses on two primary Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Guest Arrivals (Gästeankünfte) and Overnight Stays (Gästeübernachtungen) in accommodation establishments. \n\nThe data is multi-dimensional, segmented by the origin of the guests (Domestic/Inland, International/Ausland, and Total/Insgesamt). Structurally, the dataset is a 'Flat CSV' format where each row represents a specific combination of year, metric type, and guest origin. \n\nKey observations from the data include a significant dominance of domestic tourism (Inland) compared to international arrivals. For instance, in 2023, domestic overnight stays reached 98,731, while international stays were significantly lower. The dataset also captures the impact of external factors on tourism, visible in the fluctuations between 2019 and 2022, likely reflecting the COVID-19 pandemic's influence on travel patterns. The 'value' column shows high variance (standard deviation of ~35,848), which is expected as it aggregates both arrivals (lower numbers) and overnight stays (higher numbers). \n\nFrom a data quality perspective, the dataset is highly consistent. The '2_variable_attribute_code' contains missing values (NaN) specifically for the 'Insgesamt' (Total) category, which is a standard encoding practice in German official statistics to denote an aggregate rather than a sub-category. All other dimensions are fully populated. This dataset is ideal for time-series forecasting of local tourism demand and analyzing the length of stay (by calculating the ratio of overnight stays to arrivals)."
