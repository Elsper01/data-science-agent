summary="This dataset provides a longitudinal analysis of tourism activity in the municipality of Bad Berneck im Fichtelgebirge, Germany, covering the period from 2014 to 2024. It tracks two primary Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Guest Arrivals (Gästeankünfte) and Overnight Stays (Gästeübernachtungen), segmented by the origin of the guests (Domestic/Inland vs. International/Ausland). The data is sourced from the 'Monatserhebung im Tourismus' (Monthly Tourism Survey). \n\nStatistically, the dataset contains 66 observations. The 'value' column shows significant variance (std ≈ 7,441), reflecting the difference between arrival counts and the naturally higher volume of overnight stays, as well as fluctuations over the decade. A notable aspect of the data is the inclusion of 2020-2024, which captures the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and the subsequent recovery of the tourism sector. The origin 'Inland' consistently represents the majority of the volume compared to 'Ausland'. The data is structured as a flat-file time series, suitable for trend analysis, seasonality studies (though aggregated by year here), and market share comparisons between domestic and foreign tourism. Missing values are only present in the '2_variable_attribute_code' for the 'Insgesamt' (Total) rows, which is a standard structural artifact in hierarchical tourism data."
