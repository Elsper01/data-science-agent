With 66 observations, the dataset allows for a detailed examination of tourism trends, including the impact of external shocks (such as the COVID-19 pandemic visible in the 2020-2021 figures) and subsequent recovery phases. \n\nStatistically, the 'value' column exhibits high variance (Std. Dev ≈ 35,848), reflecting the inherent difference in scale between arrival counts and overnight stay counts, as well as the dominance of domestic tourism in this region. Domestic overnight stays represent the highest values in the dataset (peaking at 98,731 in 2023), while international arrivals represent the lower bound. The data is highly structured, with consistent metadata across all entries, making it suitable for time-series analysis and market share comparisons between domestic and foreign segments. Notably, the '2_variable_attribute_code' contains missing values where the label is 'Insgesamt', which is a standard representation for aggregated totals in this statistical format."
